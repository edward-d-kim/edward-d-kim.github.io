    Getting in<\/span><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      \n
    • General information about entering a country (is a visa required?) is found at IATA Travel Centre<\/a> and through a tool provided by Delta Airlines<\/a>. US Citizens can find information by typing in the name of the country in the Learn About Your Destination box at the U.S. Department of State<\/a>.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • Even for citizens who can travel to countries without a pre-arranged visa (needing contact of a consulate\/embassy), more and more countries are moving to requiring a pre-travel authorization. Those who can visit the USA<\/strong> visa-free through the Visa Waiver Program need to fill out an ESTA<\/a>. Travelers to Canada<\/strong> may need to complete an eTA<\/a>. Travelers to Australia <\/strong>may need to complete an ETA<\/a>. Travelers to South Korea<\/strong> may need to complete a K-ETA<\/a>. Starting in 2024, travelers to the European Union<\/strong> may need an ETIAS<\/a>.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
    • Many (but not all) countries in Europe are in a common travel zoned called the Schengen Area<\/a>. Iceland, Norway, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein are not in the European Union but are in the Schengen Area. The United Kingdom was in the European Union, but was never in the Schengen Area. Ireland is in the European Union, but never has been in the Schengen Area (and does not have plans to join). Romania and Bulgaria are in the European Union and are not currently in the Schengen Area (but are working to join). Monaco, San Marino, and Vatican City are not officially in the Schengen Area but have open borders with the area. Travel from one country in the Schengen Area to another does not (routinely) involve a passport check by immigration authorities, though an airline or an airport might check passports for the sake of identity. For those who have visa-free access for vacation purposes to the Schengen Area (for example, citizens of the USA), permission is granted to stay 90 days in any 180-day period. (So, if one is in the Schengen Area for 89 days, leaving to the United Kingdom for a week and coming back does not “reset” the 89-day visit.) Traveling from one country to another in the Schengen Area does not create any kind of “reset” either.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
